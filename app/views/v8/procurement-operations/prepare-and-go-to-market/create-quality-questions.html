Create quality questions

The quality questions should be based on what aspects are important to the school and must help to differentiate between good and poor suppliers.

Review the quality questions library (opens in new tab). These can be used as is, or tweaked to meet the needs of the school.

Questions should either ensure potential suppliers meet a mandatory threshold, or can be scored so a comparison can be made between the potential suppliers. Quality questions can be refined with the school to help them identify the best suppliers.

Help with creating quality questions

Threshold questions are mandatory requirements which all potential suppliers must meet. For example, does the supplier have the relevant certification.

Threshold questions normally start with 'You must'.

Scored questions allow for comparison between suppliers and are marked on a scale determined in the evaluation methodology.

Scored questions normally start with 'Demonstrate how you will'.

It is best to set word limits for the responses to questions.
